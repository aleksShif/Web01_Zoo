Capybara

The capybara is the world’s largest rodent, native to South America’s wetlands and grasslands. It has a stout, barrel-shaped body, webbed feet, and coarse brown fur. Highly social, capybaras live in groups and are excellent swimmers, often lounging in water to stay cool and evade predators like jaguars and caimans.
